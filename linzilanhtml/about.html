ZILAN LIN


Zilan Lin, a China-based media designer, earned her BA from the University of Washington. After graduation, she stayed in Washington State where she worked at Indeed Consulting Corporation from 2016 to 2017. During her internship, she was responsible for media creation and distribution.


In September of 2017, she moved to Japan to pursue her MA at the Keio University in Media Design. Focused on designing interactive experiences using digital media, her research has explored the issues of technologies, human-computer interaction, interactive arts, user experience, and art installation.


During her time at Keio University, she joined STEP, a group project, which collaborated closely with Ericsson. Her final individual project, AromaCue - A Scent Toolkit To Cope with Stress using the 4-7-8 Breathing Method, was published and presented at TEI 2020 (The ACM SIGCHI conference on Tangible, Embedded, and Embodied Interaction).


After completing her MA, she worked at Keio University as a research assistant. In June of 2020, she did an internship at a video production corporation in China.
